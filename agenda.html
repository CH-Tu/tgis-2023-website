專題演講 1 - Bring Humans into GIS/GIScience

主持人：曾義星 教授

演講者：蕭世綸 博士，校長講座教授，田納西大學

簡介

Dr. Shih-Lung Shaw is Chancellor’s Professor and Alvin and Sally Beaman Professor of Geography at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. His research interests cover GIS for transportation, space-time GIS, time geography, transportation planning, and human dynamics. His recent research has focused on space-time analytics of human dynamics in a hybrid physical-virtual world. Dr. Shaw is an elected Fellow of the American Association for Advancement of Science (AAAS) and a recipient of Edward L. Ullman Award for Outstanding Contributions to Transportation Geography and Outstanding Scholar Award in Regional Development and Planning from the American Association of Geographers (AAG). He currently serves as the President of the University Consortium for Geographic Information Science (UCGIS) and is the lead editor of Springer’s Human Dynamics in Smart Cities book series. He also served as Interim Associate Provost for International Education and Head of the Department of Geography at the University of Tennessee.

摘要

Conventional geographic information systems (GIS) with a focus on locations in physical space have failed to properly consider humans in its design. As many challenges (e.g., climate change, sustainable development, pandemics, social justice, etc.) we are facing today are closely related to human dynamics, GIS and geographic information science (GIScience) need to bring humans into GIS research, education, and system development more explicitly and effectively. An urgent research challenge is to come up with a transformative approach of developing human-centered GIS/GIScience. This presentation first gives several examples to illustrate the limitations of current GIS in dealing with humans and their activities. For example, how to better represent the Garden Night Market in Tainan on a map as such a place is created by human dynamics without an official boundary? Where is Google, Instagram, Facebook, Zoom, or LINE located on a map? What are the requirements of GIS to support autonomous vehicles? How do we represent various human activities and interactions in an increasingly hybrid physical-virtual world? How can we better handle human dynamics in GIS to address climate change, sustainable development, or social justice issues? This presentation will offer a new GIScience framework that can facilitate transformation of conventional GIS into human-centered GIS/GIScience.

專題演講 2 - 以高溫熱區、都市風廊、綠蔭地圖推動熱島退燒

主持人：曾義星 教授

演講者：林子平 博士，特聘教授，國立成功大學規劃與設計學院副院長

簡介

林子平為成功大學建築系特聘教授，兼規劃設計學院副院長，主持建築與氣候研究室，為國際上具高知名度及影響力的研究團隊。國際上過去常以寒冷地區的人體熱舒適喜好做為全球的基準而影響戶外環境設計，林教授挑戰這個溫帶主導的思維，建立了全球第一個亞熱帶 / 熱帶區的熱舒適基準，並以氣候地圖、熱風險地圖及建築能源耗用地圖，做為指認都市高溫、低風速、及耗能熱點的重要工具，並已納入我國綠建築與建築能效法令、縣市政府都市退燒政策。曾獲科技部「傑出研究獎」、臺灣建築學會「優秀青年建築獎」、成功大學「大學社會責任教學優良獎」其著作《都市的夏天為什麼愈來愈熱？》、《跳出溫度舒適圈》曾獲最佳少年兒童讀物、入圍台北國際書展大獎。

摘要

市區的氣溫高於郊區的現象，稱為「都市熱島現象」，除了受到先天地型的影響之外，都市內部綠地水域不足，通風散熱不良，遮蔭條件不佳、空調機具排熱，是造成都市高溫化主要原因。為了解決這些問題，成大建築與氣候研究室（BCLab）以生理特效溫度（即體感溫度）地圖來指認出都市高溫、低風速、高熱壓力的熱區，之後結合各類的氣候資料、土地利用資訊、建築與植栽三維模型，產置出都市風廊、綠地遮蔭、建築耗能等主題地圖，協助台北、台中、台南等多個城市實踐增綠多留藍、讓路給風走、遮蔭蓄熱少、節能減排熱等四項策略，並導入相關之都市與建築法令，讓城市舒適，幫地球降溫。
